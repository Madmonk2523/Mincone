General Questions

In New York, real estate transactions involve complex legal documents and significant financial commitments. An attorney protects your interests by reviewing contracts, identifying potential issues, ensuring clear title, and guiding you through the closing process. Our expertise can help you avoid costly mistakes and legal complications.

Most residential real estate closings in New York take 60-90 days from contract signing to closing. However, this can vary based on factors such as mortgage approval, title searches, inspections, and negotiations. We work diligently to keep your transaction on track.

Residential Real Estate

For residential buyers, we provide comprehensive services including contract review and negotiation, due diligence coordination, title examination, mortgage document review, closing preparation and attendance, and post-closing matters. We ensure your interests are protected throughout the entire process.

Never sign a purchase contract without having an attorney review it first. Key elements to understand include contingencies (mortgage, inspection, appraisal), deposit amount and escrow terms, closing date, what's included in the sale, and any seller concessions. Our team thoroughly reviews every clause to protect your interests.

Title insurance protects you from financial loss due to defects in the title that weren't discovered during the title search. This could include undiscovered liens, fraudulent signatures, recording errors, or unknown heirs. It's a one-time premium that provides protection for as long as you own the property.

Commercial Real Estate

Commercial real estate transactions are typically more complex than residential. They often involve more extensive due diligence, environmental concerns, zoning and land use issues, complex financing structures, and lease reviews. The negotiation process is usually more intensive, and the stakes are higher. Our experience ensures nothing is overlooked.

Environmental due diligence is crucial for commercial properties. This may include Phase I Environmental Site Assessments, soil and groundwater testing, asbestos surveys, and review of past property uses. We help coordinate these assessments and ensure appropriate protections are included in your purchase agreement.
